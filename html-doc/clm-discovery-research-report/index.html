Research scope

What I researched

A shared definition of enterprise CLM, regulatory pressure, where Vault Enterprise 2.0 helps, and where it stops.

CLM definition & lifecycle

  • 3-stage model: Inspect → Protect → Govern
  • 9 detailed lifecycle stages (13 in full reference model)
  • Policy under Govern; API as platform requirement

Where Vault is today

Steps 1–4 no Vault

1–3: No estate discovery, classification, or risk scoring — HCP inventory is Vault-issued only.
4: PKI roles/ACL scope issuance, but no CLM policy engine (no Org→Team rules, no report→act govern loop).

Steps 5–6 · Operate strong

Issue/enroll (PKI, ACME, SCEP, EST, External CA) and deploy (Agent templates) — for workloads already on Vault. This is where Vault earns its keep.

Steps 7–9 partial

7 Monitor: audit logs + HCP views — not estate-wide alerting.
8–9 Renew/revoke: strong for Vault-managed / ACME paths only; no orchestration for shadow or external certs on unintegrated systems.

The gap

What is missing today

Enterprises hurt where Vault has no estate view: shadow certs on LBs, legacy apps, third-party public CAs, orphaned installs.

No discovery

Network TLS scan, cloud LB APIs, CT correlation — not in Vault core

No human-readable inventory

Service, endpoint, owner — not serial / key_id / mount path

No standards reporting

SC-081, PCI, ISM packs; baseline + delta posture reports

No CLM orchestration loop

Report → Act → Operate → Evidence for the full estate

Vault sees

  • Certs Vault PKI issued
  • Grouped by mount, role, serial
  • Good for issuance audit

Blind spot

  • Public TLS on cloud LBs / CDN
  • Legacy manual installs
  • AD CS / third-party CA certs
  • Acquired systems, shadow IT

Operational risk

Ignoring the blind spot is not neutral: outage risk, audit failure, and manual work the organisation cannot scale to SC-081.

Summary

Research conclusion

Enterprise CLM is more than Inspect → Protect → Govern. Vault is strong as CA and secrets platform, but blind to certs it did not issue. Release 1 should prove that gap: discover, inventory, SC-081/PCI reports, alert — before we scope operate, policy engine, or Venafi parity.
